AI Strategy Playbook
Demand Responsive
Queue-weighted adaptive phases
Every approach gets a live priority score from queue length, accumulated wait time, incoming pressure, and vehicle priority. Green time expands for heavy demand and contracts when an approach is empty.
Network Coordination
Green wave corridor control
Adjacent junctions coordinate offsets so platoons released from one signal meet green at the next, reducing stop-start traffic on arterial roads and ring routes.
Grid Protection
Downstream spillback prevention
A signal avoids releasing cars into a blocked or saturated next road. This prevents junction boxes from filling and keeps cross traffic usable during congestion waves.
Priority Routing
Emergency preemption
Ambulance and fire-truck routes request early green at upcoming junctions, while nearby conflicting phases hold red long enough to clear the path safely.
Traffic Situations & AI Response
Long queue on one approach extend green, switch early from low-demand phases
Previous junction releases a platoon predict arrival and prepare green wave offset
Next road is full hold upstream red to prevent spillback
Emergency vehicle approaching preempt route and clear conflicts
Road blockage or accident reroute flow and reduce entry into blocked link
Rain or low-speed traffic increase clearance time and lower release rate
Balanced low traffic short cycles to reduce unnecessary waiting
